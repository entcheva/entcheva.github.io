Data Structures - Binary Trees

2017-01-24

During the junior developer panel at Flatiron School, when asked about typical interview questions, one of the panelists mentioned getting the task to:

“write a function that gets the depth of a tree”

I hear and read about trees a lot and I don’t really know what they are, so I thought this was a good opportunity to get into it.

On page 47 (10% into the book) of Cracking the Coding Interview, Gayle Laakman McDowell outlines the “absolute must have basics” of data structures and algorithms to know for an interview:

I want to quality mentioning this book in that while it’s really useful and comprehensive, it is geared toward what I would call the “Stanford Big Four” set - kids who graduated from Stanford and are vying to work at Google, Facebook, Apple, or Microsoft. That’s not what I’m interested in, but in any case it’s an interesting book, and it’s good to know what some other parts of the tech industry are expecting and/or bringing to the table.

So, McDowell mentions the above as being the absolute basics of CS concepts to know. Now, I also think it’s important to note that CS grads can also swing the other way - with tons of theoretical knowledge and no practical experience. I personally know CS grads who have told me they wish they had more experience. So anyway, these are all qualifiers to not make us feel bad or that we have to compare ourselves in any way - the only goal is greater knowledge.
